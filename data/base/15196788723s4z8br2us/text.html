Implicit Trust


Implicit trust is the way 99% of PGP trust works. Implicit trust happens when you sign a key. When you sign someone's key, your assigning them an implicit level of trust, and that level depends on the level at which you signed their key.


Explicit Trust


Explicit trust is when you do a gpg --edit-key on someone's key and then type trust and assign some level of trust to it. This is not the recommended way to trust other people's key.


The explicit trust is private. While signatures are public and make a statement to the world about your trust of someone, all explicit trust is in your local trust database (or "trustdb"), and on one will ever see it.


Trust Levels


Note that there are 4 levels of "signing":

0. I don't know

1. I have done no checking

2. I have done casual checking

3. I have done very careful checking and remember these are public values.

However, there are 5 levels of explicit "trust,":

1. I don't know

2. I do NOT trust

3. I trust marginally

4. I trust fully

5. I trust ultimately

and these are private.


Generally it is a good idea to trust only yourself ultimately, and gnupg often does this for you.
